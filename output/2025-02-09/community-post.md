📝 SUMMARY

This coaching call centered on YouTube growth strategies and business positioning for AI-focused creators. Brandon Hancock conducted detailed channel reviews for Vlad and Orlando, focusing on thumbnail optimization, video hooks, and clarity of outcome for viewers. Alexandra discussed a potential partnership opportunity for creating course content within an existing community, receiving guidance on pricing models and content focus. 💡 KEY INSIGHTS

Brandon emphasized that the first 30 seconds of a video determine its success, advocating for the principle of "show don't tell." He explained that displaying the final tangible outcome visually such as a chart or completed automation rather than describing it verbally keeps viewers engaged, while a confused viewer clicks away immediately.

For thumbnails, Brandon advised Vlad to simplify to three elements maximum and hire a professional editor for approximately 25 dollars per thumbnail. He suggested temporarily removing his face from thumbnails until obtaining a higher quality photo, noting the current image appeared unprofessional and was hurting click-through rates.

Regarding the relationship between titles and thumbnails, Brandon clarified that titles should be searchable and descriptive while thumbnails should sell the dream or outcome. He stressed that these should complement rather than match each other exactly, with the title answering "how do I find this" and the thumbnail answering "why should I watch this."

On content strategy, Brandon urged creators to "ride the wave" of trending topics like DeepSeek R1 and Gemini Flash 2.0. He noted that one timely video on a viral topic can outperform ten regular videos combined, and that creators have approximately a three-week window to capture audience attention on emerging trends before the wave passes.

For Alexandra's potential course partnership, Brandon recommended charging five times her hourly rate upfront rather than accepting revenue share. He argued that revenue share creates ongoing dependency and delayed payment, whereas upfront compensation acknowledges the immediate value of the intellectual property being transferred.

Orlando received specific feedback to re-record his DeepSeek tutorial using a crystal clear hook structure: "I'm going to show you how to run DeepSeek R1 completely for free inside your Make automations in three steps." Brandon advised replacing PowerPoint slides with Excalidraw or Gamma for a more modern aesthetic.

❓ KEY Q&A

Q: Should I show actual code or conceptual diagrams in the video intro to demonstrate outcomes?
A: Brandon advised showing the tangible outcome such as a finished chart, graphic, or automation result rather than code. He explained that code is a necessary evil that viewers tolerate but do not enjoy seeing, and that demonstrating the desired result creates clearer value proposition than showing intermediate technical steps.

Q: How can I salvage a video that is not performing well?
A: Brandon suggested changing the title to something specific and searchable, then waiting eight hours to measure the impact. For Vlad's underperforming video about automation, he recommended changing the title from "Automate All Your Social Medias" to "How to Repurpose Your Social Media Content with AI Agents" and updating the thumbnail to reflect the specific outcome of content repurposing.

Q: Should I accept revenue share or upfront payment for creating a course for someone else's paid community?
A: Brandon strongly advocated for upfront payment calculated at five times the hourly rate, explaining that revenue share delays income potentially until year-end and creates ongoing obligations to produce more content. He noted that with Alexandra's current email list of 437 subscribers, the revenue share would likely be minimal compared to the value of the content created.

Q: What is the difference between what should go in a thumbnail versus a title?
A: Brandon explained that the title should be searchable using how-to language that matches what users type into search bars, while the thumbnail should be outcome-driven showing the dream or benefit. He noted it is actually better if they are not identical, as the title captures search traffic while the thumbnail converts browsers into viewers.
